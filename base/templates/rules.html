Game Rules

Game Overview

Dara is a traditional West African strategy board game played mainly in Nigeria and neighboring regions. It emphasizes tactical placement, movement, and the creation of rows to capture opponent pieces.

Game Components

  • A 5×6 rectangular board with a grid of 30 intersections
  • 12 black pieces and 12 white pieces
  • Rows are formed by placing three of your pieces in a straight line

Core Rules

  • The game begins with players alternately placing their 12 pieces on empty intersections
  • Once all pieces are placed, players take turns moving a piece to an adjacent empty spot (orthogonal moves only)
  • Forming a straight row of three pieces allows the player to remove one of the opponent’s pieces
  • Removed pieces cannot be part of a completed three-in-a-row if other pieces are available

Winning Conditions

  • Reduce the opponent to two pieces
  • Block all of the opponent’s pieces so they cannot make a legal move

Additional Rules

  • Rows of three must be exactly three pieces; longer rows do not count
  • Opening and reforming a row counts as a new row
  • Only one opponent piece can be removed per row formed
  • Players must move if a legal move is available; passing is not allowed
